About

I was born in the Netherlands to a Dutch father and Filipina mother. This means grewing up in two cultures, which was truly amazing. I have always felt so much respect for both of them.

My parents taught me to treat people the same way you want yourself to be treated, and I always kept that in mind in life and working with people. I wish my mom was still alive so I could tell here what exciting new plans I have because I know she would be my strongest supporter.

I always thought tech jobs like coding wouldn't be anything for me. Whenever I used an app on my phone or shopped on a website, I would always ask myself: would there ever be a time and place where I could really understand how this is made?

A whole new world opened up for me since I started to educate myself better about coding. I started to follow a couple of awesome girls on Instagram who code and started to follow their adventures. They are truly an inspiration for me and it made me realise that I also want and can be a role model for my daughter. By showing her that a career in tech is not only for boys, but also for girls.

Thanks to a couple of motivational speeches from my boyfriend and some helpful telephone-conversations with Mike from Code Institute, I simply decided to just go for it. Starting my Full-Stack developers course in january 2020 at Code Institute, made me realise how much creativity and personal growth programming has to offer.

Work

I am a creative thinker that likes to solve problems. Some might call me a perfectionist. I am a quick learner with a natural curiosity about all techy things concerned.

In my job as nurse I have major experience with dialysis machine complexity. I believe I have the willpower needed to learn the skills a full stack developer needs.

Although I have little practical experience in the field of coding, what I do have is more than a decade of work-experience in high pressure situations in a large hospital. I think over the years, this has trained my ability to quickly assess situations and find logical solutions. I have the confidence, that I can apply these skills in coding too.
